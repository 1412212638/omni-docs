# AI Usage Notice

Effective Date: March 25, 2026

This page explains the core principles OmniRouters applies when providing model routing, API access, and related AI capabilities, and the precautions users should take when using AI outputs.

## 1. Nature of the Service

OmniRouters provides model access, routing, and developer tooling. Your prompts, inputs, context, files, and configuration may be sent to the relevant third-party model provider or inference infrastructure depending on the model, region, and feature you choose.

## 2. What You Should Keep in Mind

- outputs may be inaccurate, incomplete, biased, outdated, or unsuitable for your specific use case
- you should independently review and validate outputs before relying on them
- you should not upload personal data, sensitive data, credentials, or restricted information unless you have a valid basis and doing so is necessary
- for high-impact use cases, including employment, credit, insurance, housing, education, healthcare, legal, law enforcement, or other material-rights decisions, you should maintain human oversight and independent review

## 3. Platform Controls and Governance

To manage risk, OmniRouters may impose limitations based on model availability, third-party rules, safety needs, and legal requirements, including:

- restricting certain models, regions, or file-related features
- reviewing or blocking suspicious prompts, abusive patterns, or anomalous calls
- adjusting routing, content restrictions, or access to higher-risk capabilities

We may retain necessary logs, usage records, and audit information to support platform security, billing accuracy, and dispute handling.

## 4. Third-Party Model Rules

Different model providers may impose their own:

- terms of service
- content policies
- privacy rules
- rate limits, output restrictions, and geographic restrictions

Using OmniRouters does not remove your obligation to comply with those third-party rules.

## 5. Our AI Governance Commitments

Where appropriate, we continue to improve governance practices such as:

- evaluating sensitive or high-risk use cases
- managing model versions, integrations, and related risk
- handling abuse reports, model issues, and user feedback
- updating documentation, safety controls, and service restrictions where needed
